Weather System — Design Overview

Scope: Behavior & player impact. No code.

1) Goals

  • Make each run feel unique via believable, systemic weather.
  • Create “tension arcs” (calm → buildup → danger → relief) that affect planning and route choice.
  • Integrate tightly with survival stats, fire, water, food, and traversal.

2) Global Rules

  • Day/Night: Day 06:00–21:00 (full cycle ≈ 5 real-time minutes). Nights are colder and riskier.
  • Run Profile: Each run is seeded with a profile (e.g., dry, stormy, cold-biased) that biases frequency and intensity caps.
  • Local Cells: Route is divided into cells; cells can host different states simultaneously (clear ahead, storm behind).

3) States & Transitions

  • Clear: max visibility, light breeze.
  • Overcast: cloud cover increases; ambient light cools toward green-grey.
  • Light Rain: surfaces dampen; mild chill; buckets fill slowly.
  • Heavy Rain: visibility loss; footprints/surfaces wet; buckets fill fast.
  • Thunderstorm: lightning strikes, thunder delay based on distance; strong gusts; highest wetting rate.
  • Cold Front: temperature trend drops for minutes; arctic biomes push toward lethal cold without shelter.
  • Heat Wave: desert bias; hydration/heatstroke pressure increases; shade matters.
Storm Buildup: Clouds & darkness ramp up over ~20 seconds before rain/thunder begin, with wind-up audio & distant thunder cues.

4) Timing & Generation

  • Each mile rolls an “event window” (none / light / severe) with min–max duration and cooldown.
  • Biome weighting: Forest balanced; Desert favors heat waves & dust gusts; Arctic favors cold fronts & blizzard-like overcast.
  • Anti-streak: Prevent identical severe events from chaining unless run profile is storm-heavy.

5) Player-Facing Effects

Temperature & Comfort

  • Temp is a running value influenced by time, biome, elevation, wind, and precipitation.
  • Low temp → higher calorie burn; hypothermia risk; tents/pelts/fires counter it.
  • High temp → faster thirst; heatstroke risk; shade/hydration required.

Water

  • Buckets fill in rain or rivers; dirty water must be boiled/filtered before drinking.
  • Water left on fires can evaporate over time (waste).

Fire & Fuel

  • Logs have states: dry → damp → wet. Rain pushes state forward; heat pulls it back.
  • Wet logs are hard to ignite and burn shorter; heavy rain suppresses spread.
  • Lightning can ignite flammables even in rain (reduced chance).

Food

  • Raw → cooked → burned/ruined; rain & exposure speed up spoilage.
  • Cold slows decay; hot, wet conditions speed it up.

Wind & Visibility

  • Wind has baseline (profile) + gusts (events); gusts sway foliage and push smoke.
  • Heavy rain and storms reduce draw distance; flash/roll thunder sells strike distance (audio delay).

6) Interaction & Strategy

  • Shelter: Tents, caves, structures block wetting/cooling; fire inside shelter gives best heat per fuel.
  • Cart as Base: Can carry fuel, water, tents; more mass vs. speed tradeoffs.
  • Route Choice: Detour to camps during buildup phases; push miles during clear windows.

7) Balancing & Safeguards

  • No unwinnable loops: severe events have max duration and guaranteed lulls.
  • Spawn fairness: lightning avoids direct chain hits on the same player within a short window.
  • Tutorial mile biases “learning weather” with milder ranges.

8) Tuning Knobs (Non-code)

  • Rain start/stop fade times
  • Lightning min/max interval
  • Gust frequency & strength
  • Wet→dry recovery rates
  • Food spoil multipliers (by state)
  • Temp shift per biome/time
  • Event duration/cooldown ranges
  • Visibility reduction per state
